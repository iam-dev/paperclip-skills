# CEO Decision Brief Template

This is the primary output format. It's designed so the CEO can read the first section and make a decision, with the full debate available as supporting evidence.

---

```markdown
# Marketing Decision Brief: [Question]

**Date**: [YYYY-MM-DD]
**From**: CMO
**Recommendation**: [approve / approve-with-conditions / pilot-first / defer / reject]
**Confidence**: [1-10]/10
**Budget request**: [Total amount, with delta from current spend if applicable]

---

## CEO Summary

[3-5 sentences. The full picture without reading the debate. What's the decision, why, what does the CEO need to approve (budget, timeline, risk acceptance)? Write in the CEO's language — revenue, competitive position, risk — not marketing jargon.]

---

## Recommendation Details

**Recommendation**: [approve / approve-with-conditions / pilot-first / defer / reject]

**Rationale**: [2-4 sentences referencing specific arguments from both sides. Name which challenges were decisive and why.]

**Strategy modifications**: [What changed from the original proposal based on the debate]

**Action items**:
1. [Who] — [What] — by [When]
2. [Who] — [What] — by [When]
3. [Who] — [What] — by [When]

**Conditions / Gates**: [For approve-with-conditions or pilot-first: specific, verifiable criteria]

**Risks accepted**:
- [Risk from debate] — accepted because [reason]

**Budget impact**:
- Original ask: [amount]
- Revised ask: [amount, if changed]
- Expected ROI: [timeline and metrics]

**Review date**: [YYYY-MM-DD] — [What specifically should be evaluated]

---

## Full Debate Record

### Round 1: Growth Strategist Proposes

**Thesis**: [One-sentence marketing position]

**Strategy**:
[Channels, audiences, messaging, positioning — specific enough to execute]

**Rationale**:
1. [Argument 1]
2. [Argument 2]
3. [Argument 3]
[... up to 5]

**Timeline**:
- Phase 1: [What] — [Date]
- Phase 2: [What] — [Date]
- Phase 3: [What] — [Date]

**Budget**: [Allocation across channels/functions, expected unit economics]

**Success metrics**: [Measurable outcomes with targets]

---

### Round 2: Brand Guardian Challenges

1. **[Challenge title]** (critical/significant/minor) [category]: [Specific risk with concrete scenario]
2. **[Challenge title]** (critical/significant/minor) [category]: [Specific risk]
3. **[Challenge title]** (critical/significant/minor) [category]: [Specific risk]
[... 5-8 challenges]

---

### Round 3: Growth Strategist Defends

1. **[Concede/Counter/Partially concede]**: [Response to challenge 1]
2. **[Concede/Counter/Partially concede]**: [Response to challenge 2]
3. **[Concede/Counter/Partially concede]**: [Response to challenge 3]
[... matching each challenge]

---

### Round 4: Brand Guardian Stress-Tests

1. **[Stress-test title]** (critical/significant/minor): [Attack on mitigation or escalated challenge — with concrete failure scenario]
2. **[Stress-test title]** (critical/significant/minor): [Attack on mitigation or escalated challenge]
3. **[Stress-test title]** (critical/significant/minor): [New risk exposed by the defense]
[... 3-5 stress-tests]

---

### Round 5: Growth Strategist Final Stand

1. **[Response]**: [Address stress-test 1 — defense or fallback plan]
2. **[Response]**: [Address stress-test 2 — defense or fallback plan]
3. **[Response]**: [Address stress-test 3 — defense or fallback plan]

**Proposal modifications**: [What changed from the original proposal after two rounds of challenge]

---

### Round 6: CMO Decides

[Expanded version of the Recommendation Details section above, with full rationale]

| challengesConceded | [N] |

| mitigationsBroken | [N] |
| proposalModified | [yes/no] |
| confidenceScore | [1-10] |
```
